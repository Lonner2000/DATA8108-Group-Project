Site structure

What the main article covers

The article is organized as a conceptual walkthrough rather than a survey. It stays focused on the relationship between the two topics instead of branching into unrelated model families.

1. Core question

Why should in-context learning and generative modeling be discussed together at all?

2. ICL as adaptation

How prompts act like temporary datasets or task specifications without changing model weights.

3. Generative modeling

How generative models learn conditional distributions over structured data such as text.

4. Main connection

Why ICL can be interpreted as conditional generation plus implicit inference over context.

5. Mechanisms

Several perspectives for understanding the connection, including conditioning, latent task inference, and forward-pass adaptation.

6. Implications

What this view explains about prompting, scaling, and the emergence of task-like behavior in language models.
